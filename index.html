Could Targeted Ads Go Too Far or Too Evil?

We know that the ads we see splattered all over almost every single website we use are not selected at random. Our data collected by big social media networks are bought and sold specifically for the purposes of selling us more products. Products that companies are pushing onto us by referring to the data they have bought on us. Sometimes this might feel useful like seeing and ad from a product we had looked up before. But what about negative behavior? Behavior that you don't want to be sold on? The new difference is that instead of seeing these toxic ads on billboards they might be directly pushed to us all over the internet!

What if the algorithm that is being used to sell products to us doesn't have our best interest at heart? We have all seen problematic ads similar to the one above and on the right. But what if these ads are pushed onto the pages of people struggling with an eating disorder, specifically because they have an ED. We don't know the keywords that an algorithm might pick up to offer us up these kinds of ads. Then push us into performing our worst impulses or trigger our mental disorders...
